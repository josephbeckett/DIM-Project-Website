Timeline of Human Involvment in Climate Change

  • Start of the Industrial Revolution

    1712 is believed to be the rough beginning of the Industrial Revolution as during this year Thomas Newcomen invented the worlds first productive steam engine. The Industrial Revolution is a term to describe the process of where big companies and corperation began to mine coal and begin manufacturing goods on a large scale. This mainly began in the United Kingdom and then spread across the world, and this is where humanity began to pump large quantities of CO2 (Carbon Dioxide) CH4 (Methane) and SO2 (Sulphur Dioxide) into the atomosphere and as a result started to change the chemical composition of the Earths atomosphere
